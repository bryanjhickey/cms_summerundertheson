In 1 Peter 2:11 the King James Version describes believers as “strangers and pilgrims”. Although our earthly home may be Australia, the Bible calls us to live a different lifestyle to most Aussies. And the more we follow that command, the more we are, indeed, strangers to the world around us.

As CMS sends missionaries to cross cultures with the Gospel, to places that are not their home, so too our home as Christians really isn't here on earth, but in heaven. Whatever our role in the partnership of mission, we are called to live out Jesus‘ love and life here on earth as strangers until we are ready to return to our heavenly home.

SPEAKERS

  • Glenn Davies

    Glenn is Archbishop of Sydney and he will lead the daily Bible studies. Working through the book of 1 Peter, Glenn will expose the truth that as Christians “we are strangers.”

  • Greg Anderson

    Greg is Bishop of the Northern Territory. He spent 12 years working with indigenous church leaders at Nungalinya College and was the head of the Department of Mission at Moore College. Greg's theme is the vital importance of partnerships with indigenous churches.

  • Jude Long

    Jude is Principal of Nungalinya College, which provides theological and literacy training for indigenous Australians. She examines the gaps in understanding between indigenous and non-indigenous Christians.

  • David Williams

    David is Director of Training and Development for CMS Australia at St Andrew’s Hall, Melbourne. David’s theme is contemporary changes in missionary practice.

  • Sam McGeown

    Sam spent more than a decade as a church planter in Japan, including work with homeless people. He is now in Brisbane, engaged in cross-cultural gospel outreach and works with CMS Queensland.

  • Wei-Han Kuan

    Wei-Han grew up as a young Christian in South-East Asia. Prior to training for ministry he worked in taxation law with the Australian Taxation Office and international business consulting firm KPMG. He now leads CMS in Victoria.

ACCOMMODATION

If you are looking for accommodation locally, there are a number of options.

The Ibis Glen Waverley is 5 minutes from the conference and currently has great offers for a room in January. It is close to restaurants, shops and public transport. It is only $108 per day currently. It is a similar price to the East Burwood Motel.

If you are looking for a cheaper option then the Wantirna Caravan Park may be an option.

Deakin Burwood provides the option of short-stay accommodation at their residential facilities on campus. They have all single bedrooms, but organised into units of either 5-6 or 10 bedrooms with shared living areas and facilities, would work well for groups of friends or singles looking for accommodation.  There are also studio apartments.

Summer Salt Kids Program

Summer Salt is a program full of activities, learning, games and music for kids from Prep to Grade 6. Run by fantastic leaders there will be all sort of fun activities including bible studies, learning, games and music all built around the story of Abraham and Sarah – Trusting God! Summer Salt runs every morning between 9am to 12:45pm and then in the afternoon from 2:15pm to 3:30pm. The kids will get to meet missionaries each day at 2:15pm and hear their stories from all around the world.

We also have a preschool program for 3 to 4 year olds. For parents with babies, there is a room looking into the auditorium with full audio and vision links so that you can enjoy all the presentations. There will be activities and toys for them to play with. If your toddler needs space, there is a room with appropraite toys and activities.
